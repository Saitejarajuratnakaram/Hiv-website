Service Details



Purpose:

Resistance Mutation Analysis is a genetic test that identifies mutations in the HIV virus that can make it resistant to certain antiretroviral drugs. This test is essential for optimizing HIV treatment and ensuring that you receive the most effective therapy based on your virus’s resistance profile.

Services Offered:

Detection of drug-resistant mutations in HIV, guidance on selecting the best treatment regimen, and personalized recommendations to overcome drug resistance and improve the effectiveness of therapy.

Special Preparation:

No special preparation is required for the Resistance Mutation Analysis test. A blood sample is needed to perform the analysis, and it's typically done when you are not responding well to your current treatment regimen.

Cost:

The cost of Resistance Mutation Analysis may vary based on the extent of the test and the treatment plan needed. Contact us for detailed pricing information or check for coverage under your health insurance plan.

Location:

Available at our network of clinics and diagnostic centers nationwide. Home sample collection services are also available for your convenience.

Tele-consultation:

Following the test, a tele-consultation with one of our expert doctors is available to discuss your results, provide treatment recommendations, and answer any questions you may have about your therapy options.

Who should take Resistance Mutation Analysis?

Individuals with HIV who are experiencing treatment failure or those who have been on antiretroviral therapy (ART) for a prolonged period should undergo Resistance Mutation Analysis. It’s also recommended for patients who have started treatment but are unsure of which regimen will be most effective for their virus.

When to take this test?

Resistance Mutation Analysis should be considered when you are not responding to HIV treatment, your viral load is increasing despite ART, or you are starting a new treatment regimen to ensure it’s the most effective choice for your specific virus.

How to access Resistance Mutation Analysis?

You can schedule an appointment with a healthcare provider to discuss if Resistance Mutation Analysis is right for you. After booking, you can visit one of our clinics or opt for home sample collection to provide the necessary blood sample.
