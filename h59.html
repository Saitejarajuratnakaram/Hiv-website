Service Details



Purpose:

Subtype Testing is a critical service for identifying the specific strain or subtype of HIV that a person is infected with. HIV exists in various subtypes, and knowing the specific strain can significantly influence treatment decisions. This test helps healthcare providers understand how the virus behaves and responds to treatment, ensuring that the most effective antiretroviral therapy (ART) is prescribed for your condition.

Services Offered:

Testing to identify the subtype of HIV (HIV-1, HIV-2, or specific subtypes within these categories). The results provide critical information for customizing HIV treatment, especially in cases of resistance or when standard therapies are ineffective.

Special Preparation:

No special preparation is required for Subtype Testing. A simple blood sample is collected, and the test is often recommended when HIV resistance or treatment failure is suspected or when starting a new treatment regimen.

Cost:

The cost of Subtype Testing can vary depending on the complexity of the analysis and the number of subtypes being tested. For an accurate pricing estimate, you can contact our service team or inquire about insurance coverage and financial assistance.

Location:

Subtype Testing is available at specialized HIV treatment centers and diagnostic laboratories. We also offer home sample collection to make the process more convenient for you.

Tele-consultation:

After receiving the results of your Subtype Testing, you can schedule a tele-consultation with one of our HIV specialists to discuss your treatment options and any next steps based on the identified HIV subtype.

Who should undergo Subtype Testing?

Subtype Testing is recommended for individuals with HIV who are experiencing treatment failure or those who are starting antiretroviral therapy (ART) for the first time. It is also useful for individuals who need more tailored treatment options based on their specific HIV subtype.

When to test?

Subtype Testing is typically performed when there are concerns about drug resistance, or when a patient is not responding well to their current ART regimen. It is also conducted when identifying the specific subtype of HIV is necessary to guide treatment decisions.

How to access Subtype Testing?

You can access Subtype Testing by scheduling an appointment with one of our HIV care centers or booking directly online. We also offer home sample collection to make the process more accessible and convenient.
