Service Details



Purpose:

The Co-Receptor Tropism Check is a specialized test that identifies which co-receptors (CCR5 or CXCR4) the HIV virus uses to enter and infect cells. This test is important for individuals who are starting or switching antiretroviral therapy (ART), especially those considering the use of CCR5 antagonists (e.g., maraviroc). The result helps determine whether a CCR5 antagonist will be effective or if another treatment approach should be considered.

Services Offered:

Testing for HIV's co-receptor tropism by analyzing whether the virus uses CCR5 or CXCR4 receptors to infect CD4 cells. The results guide healthcare providers in selecting the most effective treatment regimen, especially when considering CCR5 antagonists.

Special Preparation:

No special preparation is required for the Co-Receptor Tropism Check. A blood sample is collected for analysis, and the test is recommended for patients starting new treatment or those experiencing treatment failure.

Cost:

The cost of the Co-Receptor Tropism Check varies based on the testing facility and the complexity of the test. You can inquire about pricing or check if insurance covers this test as part of HIV treatment or therapy management.

Location:

The Co-Receptor Tropism Check is available at select HIV treatment centers and diagnostic laboratories. We also offer home sample collection for your convenience, ensuring easy access to this important test.

Tele-consultation:

After completing the Co-Receptor Tropism Check, you can schedule a tele-consultation with one of our HIV specialists to discuss the results and any necessary adjustments to your treatment plan.

Who should undergo Co-Receptor Tropism Check?

The Co-Receptor Tropism Check is recommended for individuals who are starting or changing their antiretroviral therapy (ART), particularly if considering CCR5 antagonists like maraviroc. It is also advised for those who have experienced treatment failure or are seeking personalized treatment strategies based on the co-receptor usage of their HIV strain.

When to test?

This test should be performed before starting a CCR5 antagonist therapy, especially for individuals who are treatment-experienced or those failing their current ART regimen. It is also recommended during routine monitoring when changes in treatment or drug resistance are a concern.

How to access the Co-Receptor Tropism Check?

To access the Co-Receptor Tropism Check, you can book an appointment at one of our HIV care centers or schedule a home sample collection through our online booking system. Our team will guide you through the process and ensure that your results are used to customize your treatment plan.
